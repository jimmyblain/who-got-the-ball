## App Idea

The application is called "Who's Got The Ball?". It's purpose is to spur on difficult conversations regarding shared responsibility. The application takes the user down guides paths asking these difficult questions on who has responsibility over a specified topic, these are the "balls" that we carry. 

## Application Flow

The user selects a main category which holds the ball (questions). The main categories are:

- Finances
- Household Logistics
- Emotional

Within each category, generate five questions about the category. 

### Example Questions

- Finance
	- Who responsible for paying the bills?
	- Who has final say on big purchases?
- Household Logistics
	- Who is responsible for cleaning the house?
	- Who has final say on whether we can have guests?
- Emotional
	- Who can introduce difficult topics?
	- Who can end a conversation if it gets too intense?

The purpose of the questions is to determine who owns this responsibility. They should all have three possible answers:

- This is my ball
- This is my partner's ball
- We share this ball

## User Requirements

The answer to these questions should be tied to the user's account. They will need to be able to revisit these answers. 

They should be able to share these answers with others, especially their partner. After "partnering" with another user, the app should identify conflicting answers to questions (for example, both users answer that they are responsible for paying the bills). The app should elevate this is a discussion item for the users sharing their answers with each other.

Once partnered, the app should allow a user to "pass the ball" to their partner. This means taking a responsibility they answered as their ball, and requesting the other user to accept the ball instead. If the other user accepts, both profiles update reflecting the newly agreed upon responsibilities.

## Visuals

The app should be visually appealing. Even though we could be covering heavy topics, it is important to keep the mood light. The end goal is to get the answers to these questions and spur on deeper conversation.
